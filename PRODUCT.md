# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary audiences, all evaluating a major capital purchase of heavy machinery in Venezuela:

- **Mining & construction operations** — operations managers, site supervisors and fleet/procurement leads who specify and buy excavators, loaders, dozers, cranes and trucks. They care about uptime, specs, parts availability and on-site technical support.
- **Procurement / purchasing departments** — buyers comparing brands, total cost of ownership and after-sales support before signing off a large purchase order. They need credible numbers, clear product lines and easy ways to request a quote.
- **Investors & partners** — a brand-credibility audience (potential partners, OEM brands, institutional stakeholders) judging whether Mega is a serious, world-class player worth associating with.

Context of use: mostly desktop during a research/comparison phase, often returning multiple times before contacting sales. Spanish-language (Venezuela / es-VE).

## Product Purpose

Maquinarias Mega sells, distributes and supports heavy machinery for construction, mining (surface & underground) and load handling across Venezuela, under a "Minería Inteligente" (Smart Mining) positioning, backed by national coverage and the Escuela Técnica Mega.

This site is the company's flagship marketing landing page. Its job is to make Mega look like the dominant, technically credible, dependable heavy-equipment partner in Venezuela and to **convert qualified visitors into leads** — a quote request or a conversation with a technical advisor.

Success = a visitor in the comparison phase trusts Mega enough to request a quote / contact an advisor, and leaves perceiving Mega as a world-class, established operation.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **precise, technical, trustworthy** (established).

- **Voice:** expert and confident without bravado. Speaks the buyer's language — specs, capacities, uptime, support — not marketing fluff.
- **Tone:** serious, grounded, reassuring. This is a high-stakes capital purchase; the page should reduce risk, not hype.
- **Emotional goal:** confidence and reassurance. The visitor should feel "these people know machines, they're established, and they'll be there after the sale."

## Anti-references

- **Generic Bootstrap / "AI template" look** — cookie-cutter 3-column cards, default system fonts, harsh 1px gray borders, cramped spacing, symmetrical no-whitespace grids.
- **Sterile corporate stock** — soulless stock photography and empty buzzwords with no real machinery or substance. Use real equipment, real operations.
- **Overly playful / consumer** — rounded-bubbly, pastel, startup-cute aesthetics. Wrong audience; undercuts seriousness.
- Also avoid: cheap/discount "sale banner" energy or color clutter that would cheapen a capital purchase.

## Design Principles

1. **Engineered, not decorated.** Every element should feel machined and intentional — precise alignment, deliberate spacing, structural rhythm — like well-built hardware. Polish reads as engineering rigor.
2. **Specs are the story.** Lead with concrete numbers (capacities, HP, coverage, years). Data is the trust signal for this audience; make it legible, tabular and prominent rather than burying it under copy.
3. **Authority through restraint.** Borrow ABB/Siemens calm: heavy whitespace, a tight palette, one confident accent. Scale and confidence come from space and typography, not from louder color.
4. **Show real iron.** Real equipment and operations photography carries the emotional weight (Komatsu/Caterpillar playbook). Imagery does the impressing; UI stays out of its way.
5. **Always one obvious next step.** Lead generation is the goal — a clear, ever-present path to "request a quote / talk to an advisor," with trust signals (coverage, experience, support) reinforcing the decision at every scroll.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: sufficient contrast (watch orange-on-white and light text on photos — use overlays/scrims), visible keyboard focus states, semantic landmarks, alt text on all real imagery.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already honored) — motion is an enhancement, never required to use the page.
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning (e.g. active nav, form validation should pair color with text/iconography).
- Language is Spanish (es-VE); keep copy clear and jargon-appropriate for technical buyers without excluding less-technical procurement readers.
